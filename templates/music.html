🎧 It Started With A Playlist

Every Great Project Starts With Curiosity

Music has always been a part of my everyday life. Whether I was studying late at night, taking a walk, or simply relaxing, there was always a playlist that perfectly matched my mood.

One evening, while switching between different playlists, I found myself wondering...

"What if an application could understand someone's mood and recommend songs automatically?"

That simple question became the beginning of this project. Instead of creating another playlist application, I wanted to understand how recommendation systems actually work and build one from scratch.

🎯 The Problem

Millions of Songs

With countless songs available across platforms, finding the perfect song for your current mood can become overwhelming.

Traditional Recommendations

Most music platforms recommend songs based on listening history, popularity or trending content.

My Question

Can music be recommended purely based on emotions rather than popularity?

💡 The Idea

Instead of relying on complex recommendation engines, I wanted to understand the fundamentals. The goal was to create a recommendation system that accepts a user's mood and suggests songs with similar musical characteristics.

This project helped me explore Machine Learning, feature similarity, recommendation systems and Flask deployment while building something connected to one of my biggest interests—music.

📐 Recommendation Algorithm

Why Euclidean Distance?

Choosing the right similarity algorithm was one of the most important decisions while building this recommendation system.

Recommendation systems work by finding items that are similar to one another.

After exploring different approaches, I chose Euclidean Distance because it measures the numerical similarity between songs using features like energy, danceability, tempo, acousticness and valence.

Songs with the smallest distance are considered the most similar and are recommended to the user.

Distance Formula

√((x₂-x₁)²+(y₂-y₁)²+...)
